Step 1–3 (diagnosis):
  - Metric: equal_opportunity_difference = max per-ethnicity recall - min
    per-ethnicity recall, at shared threshold t fixed on epoch 1 (here
    t=0.51 in iter 3).
  - probe_result_3 per-epoch EOD: 0.138 → 0.075 at epoch 12 (best), then
    drifts back up to 0.092–0.103 for epochs 14-20. The model finds its
    fairness optimum around epoch 12, then oscillates around it.
  - Iter-4 failure mode: the fairness regularizer (fairness_weight=0.5,
    threshold=0.5) pulled ALL group scores upward, so recalls jumped to
    0.85–0.96 across the board. The probe's shared threshold was still
    fixed at t=0.51 from epoch 1 of that run, but the post-regularization
    score distribution had shifted so far above t that the per-group
    recall *dispersion* (which is what EOD actually measures) widened
    rather than narrowed — asian hit 1.0 recall while others sat at
    0.85, giving EOD ~0.15. The regularizer moved the global score
    distribution but did not specifically tighten per-group TPR parity
    at the fixed threshold. Confirmed by probe_result_4: tail_mean=0.1205
    vs iter-3's 0.0966.
  - Iter-3's residual gap is not a wrong-loss problem; it is a
    convergence-stability problem. With LR=1e-3 constant for 20 epochs,
    the linear separator reaches a near-optimal fairness point around
    epoch 12 and then keeps taking full-size steps that perturb the
    boundary slightly each epoch. tail_mean averages epochs 16-20, so
    it picks up exactly this post-optimum oscillation.
