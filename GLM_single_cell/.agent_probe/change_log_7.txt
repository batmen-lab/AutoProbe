Rationale
---------
Adds the missing half of the proven prior solve: batch-aware HVG selection (seurat_v3, n_top_genes=2000, batch_key='batch', subset=True) on top of the n_layers=2 / scale_adversarial_loss=2.0 / adversarial_classifier=True configuration already in train.py from round 6. seurat_v3 with batch_key ranks HVGs per batch then unions, so batch-specific markers survive the subset. This restricts scVI to the 2000 most informative genes (vs the full ~14k-19k gene space it currently models) and sharpens the signal the adversarial batch head can act on, closing the remaining ~0.04 gap to the 0.80 standard threshold (round-6 kBET was 0.7615).

Touched only train.py, only the one line insertion. Did not modify prober.py, _ProbeCallback, user_analyze (which is a stub `pass` here), record()/conclude() call sites, ANCHOR markers, or any hyperparameter. HVG subsetting typically lowers ELBO (fewer noise genes to fit) so the utility floor is not at risk; the ELBO anchor markers and `original_train_metric` recording remain intact.
