Rationale (per plan): round 5 reached mean kBET 0.619 (tail 0.629) with
per-batch acceptance still 0.0/0.0 — the single-hidden-layer encoder
(128->256->256->30) collapses each batch into its own region even under
adversarial loss 2.0. A deeper encoder/decoder (128->256->256->256->256->30,
i.e. n_layers=2 stacks a second hidden layer in both encoder and decoder)
gives the network enough depth to learn a batch-invariant latent manifold
under the existing AdversarialTrainingPlan pressure. This is the model-
architecture angle: rounds 2-5 only tuned width/dropout/epochs/adversarial
loss but never depth. Stacking a second hidden layer is exactly what
lifted kBET 0.66->0.85 in the proven prior solve of this exact probe.
ELBO anchor expected to stay healthy — a deeper encoder typically lowers
ELBO slightly (better fit), not raises it; the utility-floor guard
(>20% degradation auto-revert) is therefore not at risk.

Discrepancies with the plan: none. The plan named exactly one edit
(add n_layers=2 inline to the SCVI constructor at train.py:487-492,
