Change log for round 3
======================

Plan title: Train 50 epochs with KL warmup and lr decay

Applied edits (train.py):

1. build_parser defaults:
   --train-epochs  : 10 -> 50
   (added new arg) --lr  type=float  default=1e-3

2. main() vae.train(...) kwargs: added
   early_klwarmup=5
   early_epoch_klwarmup=5
   plan_kwargs={"lr": args.lr, "n_epochs_kl_warmup": 5}

Other args (n-latent, n-hidden, dropout-rate, max-cells, batch-balance-strength,
accelerator, devices, seed, batch-size) left unchanged from prior round values.

Rationale (per plan): scVI had not converged after 10 epochs (kBET flat at
~0.26, per-batch acceptance 0.0/0.0 — encoder still perfectly separates
batches). The fix is to extend training dramatically with proper KL warmup
and an explicit lr via plan_kwargs. scVI's batch-correction head only begins
to align batches once the ELBO has converged AND the KL warmup has finished
(the batch head depends on a well-formed latent prior). The canonical
scib-metrics PBMC recipe uses early_klwarmup + n_epochs_kl_warmup of ~5 with
~50 epochs. Expected outcome: by epoch ~30 scVI typically reaches kBET
acceptance of 0.7-0.85 on PBMC; per-batch acceptance should rise to >0.5.
ELBO anchor will continue to drop monotonically, satisfying the utility
floor.

record()/conclude() call lines: untouched.
- _ProbeCallback.on_train_epoch_end still calls prober_record(int(trainer.current_epoch), self.vae, self.adata).
- prober_conclude(0.80, 0.55, anchors=_anchors) still passes BOTH threshold values (standard=0.80, acceptable=0.55) and the elbo anchors.
prober.py: untouched.
user_analysis(): untouched (still empty pass-through).
ANCHOR markers and original_train_metric recording: untouched.

Discrepancies with the plan: none. The plan named --train-epochs default
change, a new --lr argparse arg, and three vae.train kwargs
(early_klwarmup=5, early_epoch_klwarmup=5, plan_kwargs={'lr': 1e-3,
'n_epochs_kl_warmup': 5}). All applied verbatim; the only minor
interpretation was wiring plan_kwargs['lr'] to args.lr (the new argparse
arg) rather than a hard-coded 1e-3, which is the canonical scib-metrics
PBMC recipe and matches the plan's intent of "pass it via
plan_kwargs={'lr': args.lr}".
