Iteration 10 change log
======================

Comment actioned: potential_improvement_5 (VALID_QUANTILE 0.9 -> 0.85)

Change made:
- Changed VALID_QUANTILE from 0.9 to 0.85 (a discrete/categorical
  switch to the comment's only named value; the validation tail
  grows from the last 10% of dates to the last 15%).
- Updated the trailing note on the assignment line to document
  the change ("applied round 10; was 0.9").
- No change to N_ESTIMATORS (still 5), NUM_LEAVES (still 127),
  LEARNING_RATE, FEATURES, Sales_log target, the _rmspe_lgb eval
  metric, the record()/conclude() call sites, or any other
  comment's referenced code.

Why this is expected to move the metric in the better direction:
Round 9 (NUM_LEAVES 63 -> 127) produced a clearly-positive delta:
tail_mean dropped 0.4225 -> 0.4136 (~2.1% drop). Per the recent-
progress check, "prior delta is clearly positive" -> stay incremental.
NUM_LEAVES is now at the upper end (127) of comment 3's 63-127 target,
so continuing on that lever would either exceed the comment's range
or produce diminishing returns. Switching to a different comment
preserves traceability.

The remaining untried comments are 2/9 (raise N_ESTIMATORS to
1500-2000 -- skipped, violates the epoch cap), 4 (lower
LEARNING_RATE to 0.03-0.05 -- would slow an already under-fit
5-round model, likely a regression), and 5 (VALID_QUANTILE 0.85,
an untried discrete change).

Comment 5 is the safest untried lever. The current 0.9 quantile
puts the validation split relatively early in the tail; moving to
0.85 expands the validation window to include more of the Nov/Dec
peak period where Promo2 and Christmas seasonality effects are
strongest. With 127-leaf trees now in place the model has the
capacity to fit those seasonal effects in the training partition,
and a larger validation tail gives the per-epoch RMSPE trajectory
more open-store rows (esp. Dec) on which the seasonal fit can be
evaluated. Because this is a discrete/categorical change to the
comment's only named value, it is applied in full per the
always-fully-applied rule.

Caveat acknowledged: changing the validation split reshapes the
validation tail, so cross-round tail_mean comparisons are no longer
perfectly apples-to-apples. This is a one-time, bounded confound
that resolves in subsequent rounds once the new split is in place,
and the orchestrator's revert-on-regression logic still applies
against the running best tail_mean. The orchestrator reverts if
0.85 produces a regression, so the downside is bounded.
