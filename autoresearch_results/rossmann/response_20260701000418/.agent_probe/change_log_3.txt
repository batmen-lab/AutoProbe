Iteration 3 change log
======================

Comment actioned: potential_improvement_10 (prediction clipping)

Change made:
- After model.predict(va[FEATURES]), wrapped pred with
  np.clip(pred, 0.0, float(tr["Sales"].max())) before computing val_rmspe.
- No change to N_ESTIMATORS (still 5), NUM_LEAVES (still 8), FEATURES,
  or the record()/conclude() call sites.

Why this is expected to move the metric in the better direction:
RMSPE weights relative errors symmetrically, so a single small-sales row
where the model overshoots by a few thousand units contributes a huge
squared term. The 5-round LGBM with only 8 leaves still produces a fat
right tail on predictions for low-sales days (e.g. stores with little
traffic, post-holiday doldrums); clipping predictions to the training
max observed Sales bounds that overshoot and shaves the worst-case RMSPE
contributions. This is the smallest single-edit lever that targets the
RMSPE tail directly without touching the epoch cap.

Step magnitude rationale (per recent-progress check):
Round 2 delta was clearly positive (tail_mean 0.5172 -> 0.5092, a
~1.5% drop; final epoch 0.5036 -> 0.491, ~2.5% drop) and the prior
edit was small (NUM_LEAVES 2 -> 8). Regime B + clearly-positive prior
delta means stay incremental and pick the smallest traceable edit.
Clipping is a categorical/boolean-style toggle (always-fully-applied),
so the full clip is applied in one move, but the change itself is
tightly scoped to one line of post-processing — no risk of overshooting
the boosting dynamics, and the orchestrator reverts if it does nothing.
